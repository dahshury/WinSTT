Press your hotkey, speak, and WinSTT pastes the polished text straight into whatever you
were typing in — a chat box, an editor, a terminal, a search bar. Everything between the
hotkey and the paste happens on your machine.

## The dictation loop

Every recording mode — <ModeBadge mode="ptt" />, <ModeBadge mode="toggle" />,

<ModeBadge mode="listen" />, and <ModeBadge mode="wakeword" /> — runs the same
four steps. Only the start trigger differs; the transcribe → cleanup → paste
tail is identical.

<Video
  src="dictation-loop"
  chrome="card"
  alt="A WinSTT workflow diagram showing the hotkey trigger, live preview, final accurate pass, and paste delivery at the cursor."
  caption="Every mode shares the same tail: preview, final pass, optional cleanup, paste."
/>

<StepFlow>
  <Step title="Trigger a recording">
    Press your **Push-to-Talk** hotkey (default <Combo keys="LCtrl+LMeta" />
    ). In push-to-talk you hold it; in toggle you tap to start; in wake-word you
    say the keyword; in listen WinSTT captures system audio. A short chime
    confirms the start (off in listen/wake-word modes), and the recording
    overlay appears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak — watch the live preview">
    A small, fast realtime model streams a rough preview of your words as you
    talk, so you can see it's hearing you. This preview is *not* the final text
    — it's a progress indicator. It can show in the app window, the overlay
    pill, both, or nowhere (see
    <a href="#live-transcription-display">Live-transcription display</a>).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop">
    **Release** the key (push-to-talk), **press again** (toggle), or just **stop
    talking** — the Smart Endpoint and post-speech silence timer end the
    utterance automatically after `0.7s` of silence (default). The overlay shows
    a brief "thinking" state while the main, accurate model produces the final
    transcription.
  </Step>
  <Step title="(Optional) LLM cleanup, then paste">
    If you've enabled [LLM cleanup](/docs/settings/llm), the final text runs
    through your chosen modifier (tone, translate, custom) before delivery.
    WinSTT then pastes the result at your cursor — and optionally presses Enter
    to submit.
  </Step>
</StepFlow>

<Callout type="info" title="Cancel mid-flight">
  Press <Kbd>Escape</Kbd> to abort an in-progress recording, the transcription,
  or the LLM pass. Hold the hotkey and tap <Kbd>↑</Kbd> to cycle the recording
  mode without opening settings.
</Callout>

## How text gets pasted

WinSTT delivers text the same way a fast typist's clipboard does, and falls back when an
app refuses the clipboard.

<StepFlow>
  <Step title="Clipboard + atomic Ctrl+V (primary)">
    WinSTT puts the transcription on the clipboard, fires a single{" "}
    <Combo keys="Ctrl+V" />, then restores your previous clipboard contents.
    This is one atomic paste — the whole sentence appears at once, matching the
    behavior users expect from modern dictation tools. No more "typing bit by bit."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Per-character injection (fallback)">
    If an app blocks programmatic paste, WinSTT types the text
    character-by-character through the platform's synthetic text-input path.
    On Windows, that path uses Unicode key events (`KEYEVENTF_UNICODE`).
    Slower, but it works in fields that reject <Combo keys="Ctrl+V" />.
  </Step>
</StepFlow>

<Callout type="info" title="Paste guard">
  WinSTT suppresses the synthetic keystrokes it injects for ~50 ms so its own
  <Combo keys="Ctrl+V" /> doesn't re-trigger your push-to-talk combo. You never
  see this; it just means dictation won't recurse on itself.
</Callout>

### Auto-submit after paste

Found under **Settings → General → Paste Behavior**. Off by default to preserve the
classic "paste and let me review" behavior.

<Video
  src="auto-submit"
  chrome="card"
  alt="A dictated message lands in a chat box, then WinSTT presses Enter and the message sends on its own."
  caption="After the paste lands, WinSTT presses Enter and the message sends."
/>

<SettingRow
  label="Auto-Submit After Paste"
  settingKey="general.autoSubmit"
  default="false"
>
  When on, WinSTT injects a submit key right after each successful paste — so a
  dictated chat message ships without a separate Enter press.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow
  label="Submit Key"
  settingKey="general.autoSubmitKey"
  default="Enter"
>
  Which combo to inject: **Enter** works for most chat boxes and forms;
  **Ctrl+Enter** is the convention in IDEs and chat apps (Cursor, ChatGPT
  desktop) where plain Enter inserts a newline. Only shown when Auto-Submit is
  on.
</SettingRow>

### Re-paste the last transcription

Lost the text, or pasted into the wrong window? Re-inject the most recent transcription
without speaking again.

<SettingRow
  label="Re-paste Key"
  settingKey="general.repasteHotkey"
  default="LCtrl+LShift+V"
>
  An **exclusive** global shortcut (it swallows the combo system-wide) that
  re-pastes your last transcription via the same clipboard +{" "}
  <Combo keys="Ctrl+V" /> path as dictation. Silent if nothing has been
  transcribed yet. Set it to empty to disable. If it collides with your
  push-to-talk key it's reset to default at startup.
</SettingRow>

## Context-awareness (caret split)

WinSTT can read the focused window's text right before each dictation and feed it to your
LLM cleanup pass, so names and jargon get spelled the way they already appear on screen.
On the dictation path it also reads the text immediately **before and after** your caret
(the caret split), giving the model the sentence it's continuing.

<SettingRow
  label="Context awareness"
  settingKey="general.contextAwareness"
  default="false"
>
  Reads focused-window text through the platform's accessibility APIs immediately
  before each dictation and includes it in the LLM cleanup prompt. Off by default — the
  in-app dialog requires explicit opt-in, since it can read emails, chats,
  editors, and browsers.
</SettingRow>

<Callout type="warn" title="Only used by the LLM cleanup pass">
  Context-awareness does nothing on its own. It requires the **Dictation
  post-processing LLM** to be enabled with a configured model — the captured
  window text is used only in that cleanup prompt, never stored or sent anywhere
  else.
</Callout>

<SettingRow
  label="Context deny-list"
  settingKey="general.contextDenyList"
  default="empty"
>
  Apps and websites that should **never** be read for context. Add executable
  names (e.g. `1password.exe`) or URL hosts (e.g. `bankofamerica.com` —
  subdomains match automatically). On a match, only the harmless window title is
  kept. Password fields are always excluded regardless of this list.
</SettingRow>

<Callout type="success" title="Privacy">
  With context-awareness off (the default), nothing about your screen is read.
  With it on, text goes only to your configured cleanup provider — local Ollama,
  or remote OpenRouter if you chose it. There's no per-app allowlist; the
  deny-list is the override.
</Callout>

## Live-transcription display

The realtime preview is a fast-model approximation that updates as you speak. Choose where
it shows under **Settings → General → Display**.

<SettingRow
  label="Live transcription"
  settingKey="general.liveTranscriptionDisplay"
  default="both"
>
  Where the live preview appears: **In app** (main window only), **In overlay**
  (the floating pill only), **Both**, or **None**. The overlay options require
  the recording overlay to be enabled and the realtime model to be on.
</SettingRow>

The final, pasted text always comes from the **main** model regardless of this setting —
the preview is purely a "WinSTT is hearing you" signal. See
[Real-time preview](/docs/settings/quality) for how the streaming/committed-text window
works.

## Recording overlay

A small pill floats above whatever you're working in while you record, showing an elapsed
timer, the audio visualizer, the live preview, and a thinking indicator. Two layouts are
available.

    chrome="card"

<SettingRow
  label="Recording Overlay"
  settingKey="general.showRecordingOverlay"
  default="on, xs"
>
  A 6-step slider — **Off**, then five sizes (XS / SM / MD / LG / XL). Controls
  whether the pill shows and how large it is. Greyed out in listen mode.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow
  label="Overlay Mode"
  settingKey="general.overlayMode"
  default="floating-bottom"
>
  **Floating bottom** (two-piece pill) or **Dynamic island** (docked
  top-center). Greyed out if the overlay is off.
</SettingRow>

<Callout type="info" title="Platform default">
  The overlay defaults to a bottom position on Windows and macOS, and to
  **none** on Linux (compositor quirks). You can override the position to top,
  bottom, auto, or none.
</Callout>
