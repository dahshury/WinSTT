The **General** tab is where you choose how recording is triggered, how WinSTT looks while
you dictate, and how it starts with Windows. It is the busiest settings tab — and several
controls appear or hide depending on the recording mode you pick.

## Recording

This section sets the trigger strategy and the feedback you get while dictating. The first
control swaps the rest of the section in and out.

<Screenshot src="sec-general-recording" alt="The Recording section: the four-way recording-mode switcher, the System Audio Reduction ducking slider, and the Recording Sound toggle with its Sound Library." chrome="card" caption="The Recording section — mode switcher, volume ducking, and the recording-sound library." />

<SettingRow label="Recording Mode" settingKey="general.recordingMode" default="ptt">
  A four-way switcher: <ModeBadge mode="ptt" /> <ModeBadge mode="toggle" />{" "}
  <ModeBadge mode="listen" /> <ModeBadge mode="wakeword" />. Push-to-talk holds to record;
  toggle starts/stops on a tap; listen transcribes system audio from a loopback device;
  wake word arms a keyword detector. Switching mode shows or hides the mode-specific
  controls below. See [Recording modes](/docs/recording-modes) for when to use each.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Manual Toggle Stop" settingKey="general.manualToggleStop" default="false">
  Shown only in **Toggle** mode. When on, a toggle session runs continuously from the first
  press to the second — silence-based auto-stop and silence-timing punctuation tuning are
  both disabled. Use it for long-form dictation where soft pauses were cutting you off.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Loopback Device" settingKey="general.loopbackDeviceIndex" default="null">
  Shown only in **Listen** mode. Picks which WASAPI loopback (system-audio) device is
  transcribed. `null` uses the default render device. This lets WinSTT caption a call,
  video, or anything playing through your speakers.
</SettingRow>

<Screenshot src="settings-general-listen" alt="The General tab in Listen mode — the loopback device picker and the Speaker Diarization toggle replace the microphone controls." chrome="card" caption="Listen mode swaps the mic controls for a loopback device picker and a diarization toggle." />

### Wake-word controls (Wakeword mode)

These three controls appear only when the mode is **Wakeword**.

<Screenshot src="settings-general-wakeword" alt="The General tab in Wake Word mode — the wake-word keyword selector, the sensitivity slider, and the detection-timeout control." chrome="card" caption="Wake Word mode exposes the keyword, its sensitivity, and a post-trigger timeout." />

<SettingRow label="Wake Word" settingKey="general.wakeWord" default="alexa">
  A keyword that arms recording when spoken. The list unifies 18 free keywords across two
  engines — a `2x` badge means both Porcupine (`PVP`) and openWakeWord (`OWW`) agree
  (highest accuracy); single-engine keywords carry just the `PVP` or `OWW` badge. The
  detector backend is chosen automatically from the keyword you select.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Wake Word Sensitivity" settingKey="general.wakeWordSensitivity" default="0.6">
  A 0.00–1.00 slider in 0.05 steps (21 positions). **Lower** is stricter — fewer false
  triggers, may miss soft pronunciations. **Higher** is more permissive. 0.60 is a sensible
  middle for most voices.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Wake Word Timeout" settingKey="general.wakeWordTimeout" default="5">
  Seconds the gate stays armed after a detection (1–30). If you say the wake word but never
  follow up, the engine returns to listening after this window — protection against stray
  noise triggering a long-tail recording.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Speaker Diarization" settingKey="general.speakerDiarization" default="false">
  Shown only in **Listen** mode. Colors each speaker in the transcript and tracks identities
  across the session. First use downloads ~32 MB of ONNX models. Toggles live — no restart.
</SettingRow>

### Audio feedback

<SettingRow label="System Audio Reduction" settingKey="general.systemAudioReductionWhileDictating" default="0">
  Hidden in **Listen** mode. A 6-step slider — **Off, 20%, 40%, 60%, 80%, Mute** — that ducks
  your speaker volume while you dictate so playback doesn't bleed into the mic. Intermediate
  values drop to `(100 − value)%` of the previous level; the original volume is restored when
  recording stops.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Recording Sound" settingKey="general.recordingSound" default="true">
  Hidden in **Listen** mode. Plays a short chime when recording starts and stops. Turning it
  on reveals the **Sound Library** below.
</SettingRow>

<Callout type="info" title="Custom sound library">
  With Recording Sound on, the **Sound Library** lists the built-in chime plus any clips you
  add. Drag in or browse for a `.wav`/`.mp3` (≤ 3 s), then **rename**, **preview**,
  **select**, or **delete** each entry. The active clip is stored as
  `general.recordingSoundPath` (empty = built-in); your uploads live in
  `general.recordingSoundLibrary`. The chime plays through the output device set on the
  [Audio tab](/docs/settings/audio) (`general.outputDeviceId`).
</Callout>

## Display

This section controls the interface language and everything you see while recording.

<Screenshot src="sec-general-display" alt="The Display section: the display-language picker, the visualizer-type switcher, and the recording-overlay size slider." chrome="card" caption="The Display section — interface language, visualizer style, and the recording overlay." />

<SettingRow label="Display Language" settingKey="useLocaleStore" default="en">
  The interface language, independent of the language you dictate in. Six locales are
  fully translated — **Arabic (العربية)**, **English**, **Spanish (Español)**,
  **French (Français)**, **Hindi (हिन्दी)**, and **Chinese (中文)**. Arabic renders
  right-to-left. (Additional seeded locales appear in the list but still display English
  until their translation passes land.)
</SettingRow>

### Visualizer

The visualizer is the animated audio meter shown in the main window and the recording
overlay. Pick the style that suits you — all five react live to your mic input.

<SettingRow label="Visualizer Type" settingKey="general.visualizerType" default="bar">
  A five-way switcher: <b>Bar</b>, <b>Grid</b>, <b>Radial</b>, <b>Wave</b>, and <b>Aura</b>. The
  gallery below shows each reacting to speech.
</SettingRow>

  Shown only when the type is **Bar**. The number of bars, **3–21 in odd steps** (3, 5, 7 …

</SettingRow>

### Recording overlay

<SettingRow label="Recording Overlay" settingKey="general.showRecordingOverlay" default="on, xs">
  A 6-step slider — **Off · XS · S · M · L · XL** — that both turns the floating overlay on
  and sets its size. Position 0 (Off) hides the overlay and reverts overlay-only live-display
  choices to in-app; XS–XL pick the visualizer height. Greyed out in **Listen** mode (the
  overlay never shows there). Size is stored as `general.visualizerSize`.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Overlay Mode" settingKey="general.overlayMode" default="floating-bottom">
  Greyed out when the overlay is off. Two layouts (shown below):{" "}
  <b>Floating bottom</b>{" "}
  — a two-piece pill near the bottom of the primary display; and{" "}
  <b>Dynamic island</b>{" "}
  — a morphing capsule docked to the top-center that grows as the live preview fills in.
</SettingRow>

<MediaGrid cols={2}>
  <Video src="overlay-floating" alt="The floating-bottom recording overlay — a rounded pill with the audio visualizer near the bottom of the screen." chrome="card" caption="Floating bottom — a two-piece pill at the bottom." />
  <Video src="overlay-island" alt="The dynamic-island recording overlay — a morphing capsule docked at the top-center showing live transcription above a visualizer." chrome="card" caption="Dynamic island — a top-center notch with a timer." />
</MediaGrid>

<SettingRow label="Live Transcription Display" settingKey="general.liveTranscriptionDisplay" default="both">
  Where the live preview renders, as two checkboxes — **In app** (the main-window feed) and
  **In pill** (inside the overlay). The combination maps to `none`, `in-app`, `in-pill`, or
  `both`. The "In pill" option is disabled while the overlay is off.
</SettingRow>

<Callout type="info" title="Overlay position is platform-derived">
  `general.overlayPosition` (`auto`/`none`/`top`/`bottom`, default `auto`) gates which screen
  edge the pill may appear on. On Windows and macOS `auto` resolves to the bottom edge; on
  Linux it resolves to `none` because some compositors break the paste pipeline when an
  always-on-top window appears mid-keystroke.
</Callout>

## Startup

How WinSTT behaves at sign-in and when you close the window.

<Screenshot src="sec-general-startup" alt="The Startup section: Start on Login, Start Minimized, Minimize to Tray, and Send Crash Reports toggles." chrome="card" caption="The Startup section — sign-in launch, tray behavior, and crash reporting." />

<SettingRow label="Start on Login" settingKey="general.autoStart" default="false">
  Launch WinSTT automatically when you sign in to Windows.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Start Minimized" settingKey="general.startMinimized" default="false">
  Start hidden in the system tray instead of opening the main window.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Minimize to Tray" settingKey="general.minimizeToTray" default="true">
  Closing the window keeps WinSTT running in the tray rather than quitting. With this off, the
  close button exits the app entirely.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Send Crash Reports" settingKey="general.sendCrashReports" default="true" restart>
  Opt-out anonymized crash/error reporting via Sentry. Audio and transcripts are never sent —
  only stack traces and error context.
</SettingRow>

<Callout type="restart">
  Toggling **Send Crash Reports** takes effect on the next launch. Sentry's `init()` can't be
  cleanly reversed at runtime, so both the renderer and main process read this flag once at
  startup.
</Callout>

## Related General settings on other tabs

A handful of controls live under the same `general.*` schema but are surfaced on the tab
that fits them best:

| Setting | Key | Default | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|---|
| Auto-submit after paste | `general.autoSubmit` | `false` | [Quality](/docs/settings/quality) |
| Auto-submit key | `general.autoSubmitKey` | `enter` | [Quality](/docs/settings/quality) |
| Context awareness | `general.contextAwareness` | `false` | [Quality](/docs/settings/quality) |
| Context deny-list | `general.contextDenyList` | 6 password managers | [LLM cleanup](/docs/settings/llm) |
| History max entries | `general.historyMaxEntries` | `1000` | [History](/docs/transcription-history) |
| Recording retention | `general.recordingRetention` | `cap` | [History](/docs/transcription-history) |
| Filler filtering | `general.filterFillers` | `true` | [Dictionary](/docs/dictionary) |
| Word-correction threshold | `general.wordCorrectionThreshold` | `0.18` | [Dictionary](/docs/dictionary) |
| Pre-release updates | `general.receivePrereleaseUpdates` | `false` | About tab |
| Output device | `general.outputDeviceId` | system default | [Audio](/docs/settings/audio) |

A few highlights, with their gotchas:

<SettingRow label="Auto-Submit" settingKey="general.autoSubmit" default="false">
  When on, WinSTT presses a submit key right after the paste lands. **Auto-Submit Key**
  (`general.autoSubmitKey`) chooses the combo — `Enter` for chat boxes or `Ctrl`+`Enter` for
  IDE prompts. Off preserves the historical "paste, leave the cursor" behavior.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Context Awareness" settingKey="general.contextAwareness" default="false">
  Reads text from the focused window via Windows UI Automation just before each dictation and
  feeds it to the LLM cleanup step so names and jargon are spelled correctly. Opt-in: it shows
  a confirmation dialog before activating, and is only offered when it actually helps (a
  Whisper model or LLM cleanup is active).
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Context Deny-List" settingKey="general.contextDenyList" default="6 entries">
  An app/host allow-out list for context capture. Each entry is an executable basename
  (`1password.exe`) or a URL host suffix (`bankofamerica.com`, matches subdomains). Matching
  windows have their captured text, HTML, and URL stripped before reaching the LLM. Seeded
  with six common password managers.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="History Max Entries" settingKey="general.historyMaxEntries" default="1000">
  Cap on persisted transcription-history rows (10–10,000). The main process trims oldest on
  each insert; larger histories slow the history dashboard.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Recording Retention" settingKey="general.recordingRetention" default="cap">
  Auto-deletes saved recordings: **Never** keeps everything; **Cap** prunes recordings beyond
  History Max Entries; **3 days / 2 weeks / 3 months** are absolute age cutoffs. Cleanup runs
  at startup and whenever the policy changes — "Never" still saves, it just never prunes.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Filler Filtering" settingKey="general.filterFillers" default="true">
  Strips locale-aware filler words and collapses 3+ stutters. Consults your custom filler list
  first (`general.customFillerWords`), then a per-language table. Words that are real in other
  locales (Portuguese "um") are deliberately excluded from those tables.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Word-Correction Threshold" settingKey="general.wordCorrectionThreshold" default="0.18">
  The fuzzy-match threshold (0–1) for the deterministic corrector that runs before LLM cleanup.
  **Lower** is stricter — fewer false positives, more near-misses left for the LLM. 0.18 is the
  reference default.
</SettingRow>

<SettingRow label="Pre-Release Updates" settingKey="general.receivePrereleaseUpdates" default="false">
  Opt in to alpha/beta auto-updates. Alpha installs always keep updating to newer alphas
  regardless of this toggle; the knob only changes behavior on stable builds.
</SettingRow>

## Reset

A single button at the bottom restores **every** setting to its factory default after a
confirmation dialog.

<Callout type="error" title="Reset to Defaults has no undo">
  Confirming the reset clears all tabs — model, audio, quality, dictionary, snippets, LLM,
  TTS, and integrations — back to defaults. There is no undo; your API keys, custom modifiers,
  dictionary entries, and sound library are wiped.
</Callout>
