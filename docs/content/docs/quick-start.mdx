Go from a fresh download to text at your cursor in five steps — the onboarding wizard
picks a model and tests your mic for you, then you hold a hotkey and speak.

WinSTT is local-first. The installer bundles the speech engine and a starter model, so
the only thing you need is a microphone. Audio is transcribed in memory by on-device ONNX
models and never leaves your machine.

<Callout type="success" title="Nothing to install but the app">
  The app package ships the Rust speech engine and a vendored `tiny` model — there's
  no Python, no separate service, no model download required to get your first transcription, and no
  account to create. You can be fully offline.
</Callout>

## Five steps to your first transcription

<StepFlow>
  <Step title="Download the app">
    Download the latest alpha package from the
    <a href={latestReleaseUrl}>GitHub Release</a>. Current releases include a macOS
    Apple Silicon DMG, Linux AppImage/deb/rpm packages, and Windows portable builds.
    Not sure which one? See [Install](/docs/install) for platform notes and system
    requirements.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Launch — onboarding picks local vs cloud and tests your mic">
    On first launch the onboarding wizard walks you through three choices: run a **local**
    on-device model (the default — private and offline) or a **cloud** provider, select
    your **microphone**, and run a quick **mic test** so you can confirm WinSTT hears you
    before you dictate. Pick local and you're done in seconds.

    <Screenshot src="onboarding" alt="Onboarding wizard step showing the local-versus-cloud choice and a microphone level meter for the mic test." chrome="card" label="Onboarding" caption="Choose local for fully on-device transcription; the mic test confirms your input device works." />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick or download a model">
    The starter `tiny` model is bundled and ready, so you can skip straight to dictating.
    To choose a different one, open the model picker from the footer or the **Model**
    settings tab: it lists 40+ models grouped by maker, each row showing accuracy and
    speed bars, size, language coverage, and per-quantization download badges. Downloads
    stream in the background without interrupting the app.

    <Screenshot src="model-dropdown" alt="The open model picker: a maker rail on the left and model rows with accuracy and speed bars, size, language scope, and quantization download badges." chrome="card" label="Model picker" caption="Pick a model and quantization; downloads run in the background and most swaps are live — no restart." />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Hold the hotkey and speak">
    The default mode is **Push-to-Talk** <ModeBadge mode="ptt" />: hold <Combo keys="LCtrl+LMeta" />
    and start talking. A recording overlay floats above whatever you're working in and
    shows a live preview as the words come in. The main window's footer shows your active
    hotkey, microphone, and model at a glance.

    <Video src="overlay-island" alt="The dynamic-island recording overlay floating above the screen, showing live transcription text above a small audio-visualizer capsule while you speak." chrome="card" caption="The overlay shows the live preview while you hold the hotkey and speak." />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Release — text appears at your cursor">
    Let go of the hotkey and WinSTT runs the final, accurate pass and pastes the polished
    text straight into wherever your cursor was — a chat box, an editor, a terminal, a
    search bar. That's it: hold, speak, release.

    <Screenshot src="main" alt="The WinSTT main window: a 9-band audio visualizer with the active hotkey, microphone, and model shown in the footer." label="WinSTT — main window" caption="Between dictations the compact main window stays out of the way; the footer shows your hotkey, device, and model." />
  </Step>
</StepFlow>

<Callout type="info" title="Don't like the default hotkey?">
  Push-to-Talk is bound to <Combo keys="LCtrl+LMeta" /> out of the box. Change it — or
  switch to Toggle, Listen, or Wake-word triggering — on the
  [Hotkey](/docs/settings/hotkey) and [recording modes](/docs/dictation) pages.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warn" title="If the hotkey does nothing">
  Some locked-down corporate machines block the low-level keyboard hooks Push-to-Talk
  relies on. Switch to **Toggle** mode (it uses a single key event) or run WinSTT as
  Administrator. See [Troubleshooting](/docs/troubleshooting) for audio-device and
  download issues.
</Callout>
