Beyond Whisper and NeMo, WinSTT ships five more model families on the same ONNX engine — covering ultra-low-latency English dictation, a leaderboard-topping multilingual model, East-Asian languages, and three Russian-specialist engines. Pick by language coverage first, then by the accuracy/speed trade-off.

Every model on this page runs through the single `onnx-asr` ONNX engine, supports the live realtime preview, and is hot-swappable from the [Model](/docs/settings/model) tab — no restart, no backend to choose. Precision is set per-model via `onnxQuantization`; see [Compute Types](/docs/models/compute-types).

## Moonshine

Useful Sensors Moonshine is a raw-audio encoder built for **latency**, not raw accuracy. The tiny model is ~5.8× faster than Whisper tiny at the same scale, which makes it a strong realtime-preview and live-dictation pick. The base English models and the five language fine-tunes (Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, Vietnamese) round out the family — each fine-tune is single-language only, with no auto-detect.

Moonshine exposes the widest quantization range of any family: `""` (default), `fp16`, `q4`, `bnb4`, `int8`, `uint8`, and `q4f16`. The `int8` / `uint8` graphs are the smallest downloads (the English tiny model drops from 327 MB to 67 MB) and are the best fit for CPU-only machines.

<Callout type="info" title="The fastest realtime model">
  At 27M parameters and RTFx ~1800, Moonshine Tiny is the lightest model in the catalog
  and an ideal **realtime preview** model. Pair it with a larger main model when you want
  the live preview to feel instant without taxing the GPU.
</Callout>

## Cohere

Cohere Transcribe is a 2B-parameter Conformer encoder with a lightweight Transformer decoder. It currently sits at **#1 on the Hugging Face Open ASR Leaderboard**, with the lowest word-error rate of any model WinSTT ships (5.1). It covers 14 languages with automatic language detection.

<ModelTable
  head={["Model", "Params", "WER", "RTFx", "Languages"]}
  numeric={[1, 2, 3, 4]}
  rows={[
    ["Cohere Transcribe", "2B", "5.1", "270", "14"],
  ]}
  caption="Languages: Arabic, German, Greek, English, Spanish, French, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Dutch, Polish, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Chinese — with auto-detect."
/>

Quantizations: `""` (default), `fp16`, `q4`, and `q4f16`. The default fp32 graph is the largest single download in the catalog (~5 GB); on a GPU, the `fp16` (~2.4 GB) or `q4f16` (~1.5 GB) variants give you most of the accuracy for a fraction of the size and VRAM.

<Callout type="warn" title="Plan for the download and VRAM">
  Cohere is a 2B model. Even the smallest `q4f16` download is ~1.5 GB, and the default
  precision needs roughly 5 GB of free VRAM to create the GPU session. If the session
  can't fit, ONNX Runtime falls back to CPU — accurate, but slow at this size.
</Callout>

## SenseVoice

FunAudioLLM SenseVoice Small is a 234M Conformer encoder focused on **East-Asian languages**: Chinese, English, Japanese, Korean, and Cantonese, with automatic language detection. It ships as a single-pass `int8` graph — there is no fp32 variant — so the download is a compact 159 MB and the only available precision is `int8`.

## Russian-specialist families

Three families target Russian exclusively. They are the right pick when you dictate primarily in Russian and want accuracy that beats Whisper at a fraction of the size. (NVIDIA's Russian FastConformer models live in the [NeMo](/docs/models/nemo) family.)

### GigaAM

GigaAM v3 is a 243M Russian engine that posts the **lowest Russian WER** in the catalog. It ships in two end-to-end variants: a CTC graph (faster decoding) and an RNN-T graph (slightly higher accuracy).

<ModelTable
  head={["Model", "Params", "WER", "RTFx", "Architecture"]}
  numeric={[1, 2, 3]}
  rows={[
    ["GigaAM v3 E2E CTC", "243M", "5.5", "600", "CTC — faster decoding"],
    ["GigaAM v3 E2E RNNT", "243M", "5.0", "450", "RNN-T — higher accuracy"],
  ]}
  caption="Russian only. Both variants offer the default precision plus int8."
/>

GigaAM supports `""` (default) and `int8`. The default graph is a ~3.5 GB download; `int8` drops it to ~900 MB.

### Vosk / Kaldi

Vosk provides lightweight offline recognition built on the Kaldi toolkit. Both Russian models are small, load fast, and run comfortably on CPU — the small variant is the smallest Russian option in the catalog.

<ModelTable
  head={["Model", "Params", "WER", "RTFx", "Notes"]}
  numeric={[1, 2, 3]}
  rows={[
    ["Vosk Russian", "65M", "13.0", "350", "Full model — higher accuracy"],
    ["Vosk Russian (Small)", "23M", "18.0", "800", "Lightweight — faster, smaller download"],
  ]}
  caption="Russian only. Both offer the default precision plus int8."
/>

Vosk/Kaldi models support `""` (default) and `int8`. The full model is a 264 MB download (72 MB at `int8`); the small model is 93 MB (27 MB at `int8`).

### T-One

T-Tech's T-One is a 72M Russian engine that hits accuracy on par with the NeMo Russian FastConformer models at a much smaller footprint (144 MB).

Use this to narrow the list before weighing accuracy and speed:

  head={["You need…", "Best fit here"]}

  ]}

/>

<Callout type="info" title="Single-language models hide the language picker">
  The Moonshine fine-tunes and every Russian model are single-language — the **Language**
  control in the Model tab disappears for them, since there is nothing to detect or pin.
  Cohere and SenseVoice support auto-detect, so they keep it.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Hot-swapping vs. family switches">
  Switching between models within this group hot-swaps live without a restart. Changing to a
  different model *family* (e.g. Moonshine → Cohere) reconnects the engine — the overlay shows a
  brief "swapping" state.
</Callout>
